Chapter 3 - The Truth Continuum

In Chapter 3, Cairo discusses the concept of truthfulness in visualizations. Since visualizations are models, it is very difficult for any one visualization to be 100% truthful and accurate. Therefore, both viewers and creators contribute to errors in “truth” of data visualizations. Cairo discusses three mind bugs that our human minds create when looking at data charts and graphs:

  1. Patternicity Bug – Finding patterns in data because we are looking for them, even if those patterns don’t actually exist.
  2. Storytelling Bug – Creating a story to explain and connect the patterns our minds detect.
  3. Confirmation Bug – Interpreting all data as confirming our explanation, even if it actually contradicts it.

Cairo then moves on to talk about the mistakes made on the creation side of visualizations. He discusses a misleading journalism headline and then suggests that before we create or publish visualizations, we must stop and think. This will reduce confirmation bias / tunnel vision and also motivate to increase the depth and breadth with which we are viewing patterns and facts. Cairo concludes saying that a perfect model is unrealistic but through good practices, viewing, and application, we can certainly create visualizations that are extremely effective.
